About Harry Potter

The Harry Potter books are a fantasy series about a young orphan named Harry Potter who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard and is famous for surviving an attack from the evil wizard Lord Voldemort as an infant. The seven-book series follows Harry and his best friends, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, as they attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, navigate teenage life, and ultimately confront Voldemort in a battle for the fate of the wizarding world.


About the Author

J.K. Rowling (Joanne Rowling) went from a struggling single mother facing poverty and depression in Edinburgh to a globally renowned author by creating the Harry Potter series, conceived on a train in 1990 and written in cafes while raising her daughter, eventually finding success after numerous rejections with the first book published in 1997. Her journey, marked by personal hardship, perseverance, and immense literary triumph, transformed her life and created one of history's biggest media franchises.
